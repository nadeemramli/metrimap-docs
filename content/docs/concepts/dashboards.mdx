A **dashboard** turns the metrics on a canvas into something you review. Each canvas
has a dashboard, composed of **widgets**.

## Widgets

A **widget** shows one or more metric series. Types:

- **Chart** — line, area, bar, or pie over time.
- **KPI** — a single headline number (e.g. current MRR).
- **Table** — periods and values in a grid.

A widget draws its series from either a **tracked metric** (shared, catalogued) or a
**card** on the canvas. Arrange widgets to answer a question at a glance — for the
subscription business, a dashboard might pair an *MRR* KPI with a *New vs Churned MRR*
line chart.

## Impact badges: what's being done about a metric

A dashboard shows *what* is happening; Canvasm also shows *what bets are active* on a
metric. Metric widgets carry **impact badges** — a compact count and status of the
[Strategy](/docs/concepts/strategy-impact) actions and hypotheses linked to that
metric. Open the badge to see each linked item's owner, expected change,
measurement window, and current status, and jump to its impact trace.

<Callout title="Why this matters">
  Reviewing a flat number tells you it moved. Reviewing a number *with its linked
  bets* tells you whether your work is what moved it.
</Callout>

## Audience-specific views

Dashboards and shared views can be scoped to an **audience** so they only surface
metrics that audience is allowed to see. The same canvas can therefore power a
finance view, an exec view, and an external-partner view — each showing only its
permitted numbers. See [Group to dashboard](/docs/product-system/group-to-dashboard)
and [Groups & visibility](/docs/concepts/groups-and-visibility).

## Review cadence

Dashboards are where the weekly/monthly **review** happens: read the numbers, check
the guardrails, and note what you learned. The [Learn track](/docs/learn/dashboard-cadence)
walks through a review rhythm.
